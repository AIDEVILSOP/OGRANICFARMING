PRINCIPLES AND APPLICATIONS OF ORGANIC FARMING


Some of the most common principles of organic farming are:


  1. No use of “synthetic” chemicals – fertilizers or pesticides at their “natural origin” are allowed
  2. No use of genetically modified organisms (GMOs)
  3. Recycle all organic waste
  4. Crop rotation to improve soil regeneration
  5. Pest control by biological agents
  6. Extensive breeding with organic food and give priority to alternative medicines and preventions
  7. Ensure animal welfare (soil surfaces must be habitable, there must be outdoor courses and grazing, prohibition of breeding above the ground)
  8. Respect for the environment and preservation of natural resources
  9. Maintenance and development of biodiversity (cultivation and breeding of various species, maintaining or planting hedges)


The term organic farming can apply to the following categories of products:


  1. Unprocessed products:
    1. cereals
    2. fruits
    3. cotton
    4. flowers
    5. animals
    6. eggs
    7. milk
  2. Processed products for human consumption:
    1. cheese
    2. bread
    3. instantaneous meals
  3. Food for animals like organic soy cakes
  4. Materials for vegetative reproduction and seeds
